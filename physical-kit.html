Physical Kit

Everything you need to play Cli‑Mit Quest offline in one place.

Downloads

  • All Cards (PDF) — This PDF contains all of the question and answer cards for offline play.
  • Answer Key — Print or save this file so the host can quickly check answers during the game.
  • Printable Buzzers — Four large cards (green, blue, yellow and red) for teams to hold up when they know the answer.

Quick Setup (≈5 minutes)

  1. Print the question cards from the PDF and cut them out. Separate them into categories and point values.
  2. Print one coloured buzzer card per team. Each card shows a colour and the team name for easy visibility.
  3. Grab a timer (or use a phone) to keep track of the 10‑second reading period and 20‑second answering period.
  4. Gather pens or markers and a simple score sheet, if you want to track points offline.

How to Run the Game Offline

Set up the question cards face down in a grid by category and point value. Choose a team to start. Teams take turns picking a card and reading the question aloud. Once the card is read, give everyone 10 seconds to think, then open the floor for buzzers. The first team to hold up their coloured card gets 20 seconds to answer. Use the answer key to judge responses and award points accordingly. If a team answers incorrectly, other teams may buzz in for a chance. The card is discarded only after a correct answer or if no one can answer.

Print Tips

  • Use thicker paper or cardstock for durability so the cards last longer.
  • If available, print double‑sided so the back of each card can show the category or remain blank.
  • Trim the cards along the cut lines for neat, uniform sizes. A paper cutter works best.
  • Consider laminating or slipping the cards into protective sleeves for repeated use.

Checklist

  • Printed question cards (from the All Cards PDF)
  • Printed coloured buzzer cards (one per team)
  • Answer key (printed or saved digitally)
  • Timer or smartphone to measure reading and answering periods
  • Score sheet and pens/pencils for keeping track
  • Scissors or a paper cutter; tape or clips (optional)
